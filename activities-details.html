Catherine Falls Trek

Duration
3–4 hrs
Difficulty
Easy–Moderate
Group Size
2–8 ppl
Price/Person
₹800

A half-day guided trek through working tea gardens to Catherine Falls viewpoint. Walk alongside estate workers, learn about tea cultivation, and end at one of the Nilgiris' most spectacular waterfalls. Fullest Oct–Mar.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 7:00 am — Meet guide at town center or your homestay
  2. 7:15–8:00 am — Walk through tea gardens, learn about plucking seasons
  3. 8:00–8:30 am — Forest path with bird calls (listen for Nilgiri Flycatcher)
  4. 8:30–9:00 am — Arrival at falls viewpoint; photos & 20-min rest
  5. 9:00–9:45 am — Return walk through estate
  6. 9:45–10:15 am — Tea & snacks at estate owner's home
  7. 10:30 am — Return to town

✅ What's Included

Local guide Packed snacks Tea/coffee Route map

🎒 What to Bring

Sturdy hiking shoes, water bottle, light jacket, camera, sunscreen, rain gear (optional).

📅 Best Season

Oct–Mar for highest water levels. July–Sep is also OK but less dramatic.

🗣️ Booking

Ask your homestay to arrange. Available year-round. Book evening before for next day.

💡 Pro tip: Bring a swimsuit — if water levels are safe, take a dip in the pool below the falls. The trail can be slippery after rain, so wear good grip shoes.

Tea Factory Tour

Duration
2 hrs
Difficulty
Easy
Group Size
2–15 ppl
Price/Person
₹300

From leaf to cup in 2 hours. See how fresh-plucked tea leaves are withered, rolled, oxidized, and fired in a working estate factory. Taste the estate's fresh tea and learn why Kotagiri's elevation (1,950m) produces some of India's finest Orthodox tea.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 9:00 am — Arrive at tea estate (usually 3–5 km from town)
  2. 9:15–9:45 am — Tea plucking demonstration by estate workers (pick leaves yourself if interested)
  3. 9:45–10:45 am — Full factory tour: withering beds, rolling machines, oxidation, firing chamber
  4. 10:45–11:15 am — Tea tasting in estate parlor: taste 2–3 varieties, learn tasting notes
  5. 11:15–11:45 am — Souvenir shopping & visit estate shop

✅ What's Included

Factory guide 3 tea samples Tea-tasting notes 10% discount on shop

🎒 What to Bring

Comfortable clothes (can get dusty in factory), closed shoes, water, notebook for tasting notes (if interested).

📅 Best Time

Jan–Mar (peak production). Year-round available but slower production Apr–Jun.

🗣️ Booking

Ask your homestay. Advance booking recommended (2–3 days) especially for groups.

💡 Pro tip: Take home a pack of estate-fresh tea (₹400–600 for premium). It tastes completely different from commercial tea. The workers are proud to share — ask questions!

Shola Birding Walk

Duration
3 hrs
Difficulty
Easy
Group Size
2–6 ppl
Price/Person
₹600

Early-morning immersion in Longwood Shola forest with a trained naturalist. See 20–30 bird species including the endemic Nilgiri Pipit, Nilgiri Wood Pigeon, and Nilgiri Flycatcher. Binoculars provided. Suits both beginners and experienced birders.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 6:00 am — Meet guide at Longwood Shola main gate
  2. 6:15–6:30 am — Introduction to birds & briefing; binoculars check
  3. 6:30–8:00 am — Silent forest walk along birding trail; listen for calls
  4. 8:00–8:15 am — Stop: identify sightings, record in notebook
  5. 8:15–8:45 am — Return walk; second circuit if time permits
  6. 8:45–9:15 am — Tea/coffee & bird discussion in estate shelter

✅ What's Included

Naturalist guide Binoculars Bird ID guide Tea/coffee

🎒 What to Bring

Camera with zoom lens (optional), notebook, water, light jacket, dark/muted clothing (birds shy away from bright colors).

📅 Peak Season

Nov–Feb (most species active). Jul–Sep monsoon also good (fewer people).

🗣️ Booking

Contact: Nilgiris Naturalists Society +91 4266 221234 or ask your homestay. Book 1–2 days prior.

💡 Pro tip: Arrive 5 minutes early. Keep absolute silence during walk — the slightest sound scares birds. The best sightings are 6:00–6:45 am. Even non-birders love the shola forest's quiet magic.

Sullivan Trail Historical Walk

Duration
5–6 hrs
Difficulty
Moderate
Group Size
4–12 ppl
Price/Person
₹500

Walk stretches of the historic 1819 route John Sullivan used to enter the Nilgiris. Pass through forests, visit the site of his first Kota village encounter, and hear stories of tribal history. This trek is retraced annually on June 15 as an expedition — book early for that date.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 7:00 am — Meet guide at Sullivan Memorial or town center
  2. 7:15–7:30 am — Historical briefing: 1819 route and tribal encounters
  3. 7:30–10:00 am — Forest trek on original 1819 path (~4 km)
  4. 10:00–10:30 am — Visit Kota village site; elder storytelling (if available)
  5. 10:30–11:30 am — Packed lunch at scenic spot
  6. 11:30–12:45 pm — Return trek via alternate route

✅ What's Included

History guide Packed lunch Route map Tribal stories

🎒 What to Bring

Sturdy hiking boots (forest can be muddy), water (2L), camera, light jacket, rain gear, sunscreen, insect repellent.

📅 Special Event

June 15 Expedition — Annual re-enactment with larger groups, historical reenactors. Book by May 15 · ₹1,200/person.

🗣️ Booking

Nilgiris Heritage Trust: +91 4266 235456 or your homestay. For June expedition, book very early.

💡 Pro tip: This trek combines adventure with history — great for learning travelers. Guides often share tribal legends and colonial stories. Not just a walk; it's an immersive history lesson.

Jeep Safari to Rangaswamy Peak

Duration
4–5 hrs
Difficulty
Moderate
Group Size
4–6 per jeep
Price/Person
₹1,200

Off-road jeep adventure to Rangaswamy Peak, a sacred site of the Irula tribe, with tribal elder storytelling and panoramic views. Pass Kodanad viewpoint on the way. The 400-foot rock pillar is visible from here. For adventure seekers.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 6:30 am — Jeep pickup from town or homestay
  2. 6:45–7:45 am — Drive to Kodanad Viewpoint; photos, sunrise viewing
  3. 7:45–10:15 am — Off-road drive to Rangaswamy Peak (thrilling terrain)
  4. 10:15–10:45 am — Peak visit, tribal guide explains spiritual significance
  5. 10:45–11:15 am — Snacks & rest at peak
  6. 11:15–12:30 pm — Return drive, often different route

✅ What's Included

Jeep hire Local guide Bottled water Peak visit

🎒 What to Bring

Camera, light jacket, waterproof bag, sturdy shoes, sunscreen, snacks, water. Not for sensitive stomachs — ride is bumpy!

📅 Best Season

Oct–Mar (avoid monsoon for safety). Clear days best for views.

🗣️ Booking

Ask your homestay or approach taxi stands at Kotagiri bus station. Book evening before. Min 2 people.

💡 Pro tip: This is not for the faint-hearted — the off-road section has hairpin turns and steep drops. But the views and adrenaline rush make it unforgettable. Tribal guides are wonderful storytellers.

Tea/Spice Plantation Walk

Duration
2–3 hrs
Difficulty
Easy
Group Size
2–8 ppl
Price/Person
₹400

Morning walk through working tea and spice plantations with hands-on plucking demonstration. Learn about cultivation, seasonal cycles, and the ecosystem. Many homestays are on working farms — a gentle, locally-rooted experience.

📋 Itinerary

  1. 8:00 am — Meet farm owner/guide at plantation entrance
  2. 8:15–8:45 am — Tea plucking demo: try plucking "two leaves and a bud" yourself
  3. 8:45–9:45 am — Walk through tea gardens, spice garden (cardamom, pepper, cinnamon), learn seasonality
  4. 9:45–10:00 am — Shade rest, water, wild fruit tasting (if season permits)
  5. 10:00–11:00 am — Farm lunch: rice, sambar, vegetables, homemade pickle
  6. 11:00 am — Return or optional afternoon tea processing visit

✅ What's Included

Farm guide Plucking demo Tea/coffee Farm lunch

🎒 What to Bring

Comfortable shoes (can be muddy), light clothes, hat, water, camera. Hands get wet during tea plucking — bring a towel.

📅 Year-round

Available any time. Busiest in plucking season Jan–Mar. Mornings best for activity, afternoons for processing visits.

🗣️ Booking

Most homestays offer this. Simply ask your host — many farms are family-run and close by. No advance booking needed.

💡 Pro tip: This is the most intimate experience — farm owners often share family meals and tea. Kids love it. Not a rushed tourist activity; it's genuine farm life. Perfect for families and slow travelers.
