THE NILGIRIS · TAMIL NADU · 1,793 M

The quiet side of the Blue Mountains

Kotagiri is the oldest hill station in the Nilgiris — older than Ooty, calmer than Coonoor. Tea estates, shola forests, waterfalls and homestays run by people who grew up here.

Places that make Kotagiri, Kotagiri

Less commercial than Ooty, every spot here is a short, scenic drive from town — through tea gardens most of the way.

VIEWPOINT↗
18 km · ~40 min

Kodanad Viewpoint

A panoramic view over the Moyar valley and plains below — best on a clear morning before clouds gather.
WATERFALL↗
~8 km · Aravenu

Catherine Falls

A roughly 250-ft double cascade near Aravenu. Visit a recognised viewpoint and avoid slippery, unverified shortcuts.
FOREST↗
3 km from bus stand

Longwood Shola

A 116-hectare Ramsar wetland and shola forest with an official guided, family-friendly trail.
HERITAGE↗
Kannerimukku · 2 km

Sullivan Memorial

A restored bungalow at Kannerimukku that offers context on Kotagiri's colonial-era and indigenous history.
PEAK↗
~20 km · via Kodanad

Rangaswamy Peak & Pillar

A sacred Nilgiri landscape and striking peak, now available through an official guided forest trek.
DAY TRIP↗
Coonoor · 23 km

Nilgiri Mountain Railway

Ride the UNESCO-listed mountain railway from nearby Coonoor, with live schedules checked through Indian Railways.

When to come & how to reach

Kotagiri sits in a rain shadow behind the Doddabetta range — so it often stays clear even when Ooty is wrapped in monsoon cloud.

✨

Oct – Mar

9–22°C. Crisp air, clear valley views, low crowds in Nov–Feb. Carry warm layers for mornings.

🌸

Apr – Jun

15–25°C. Pleasant escape from the plains' heat. June 15: Sullivan Trail expedition week.

🌦

Jul – Sep

Misty & green. Lighter rain than Ooty thanks to the rain shadow — waterfalls at their best.

🍃

All year

10–25°C range. Never harsh. The classic subtropical highland climate of the Nilgiris.
