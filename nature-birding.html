Sholas & Birding

Kotagiri sits in one of the planet's great endemic-bird hotspots, wrapped around an ancient forest that quietly waters the whole town. Here's how to understand it — and where to find the birds.

Sholas & ecology

What's a shola? Kotagiri's living water-towers

Sholas are pockets of ancient montane rainforest tucked into the valleys and folds of the high Western Ghats, ringed by natural rolling grassland. Together this shola–grassland mosaic works like a giant sponge — soaking up rain through marshes and releasing it slowly through perennial streams that feed the villages below.

They're dense, multi-layered forests rich in epiphytes — moss, orchids and ferns — and they shelter species found nowhere else on Earth. Protecting the open grassland matters as much as the trees: range-restricted birds like the Nilgiri pipit depend on intact high-elevation grassland.

Longwood Shola

The last and only urban shola in the Nilgiris — a Reserve Forest and Ramsar wetland of international importance, right in the heart of Kotagiri town, and a key water source for surrounding villages.

Birdwatching

10 of 16 Western Ghats endemics, around one town

Quiet, tea-clad and with Longwood Shola in its centre, Kotagiri is one of the best bases in the Nilgiris for endemic birds. Early mornings are prime; a local guide dramatically improves sightings of the skulking shola species.

Endemic & star species to look for

Nilgiri Sholakili (Blue Robin) Nilgiri Laughingthrush Nilgiri Wood-Pigeon White-bellied Sholakili Nilgiri Flycatcher Black-and-orange Flycatcher Nilgiri Flowerpecker Painted Bush Quail Tytler's Leaf Warbler Nilgiri Pipit (grassland) Winter: Kashmir Flycatcher Winter: Indian Blue Robin

🗺️ Best spots around Kotagiri

  • Longwood Shola — White-bellied shortwing, Nilgiri laughingthrush, wood-pigeon
  • Kodanad estate road — bird en route to the viewpoint, through tea & shola edge
  • Tea-garden hill edges — reliable for the black-chinned laughingthrush

📋 Plan your birding

  • eBird hotspots: Kotagiri — General Area & Franciscan Brothers Road
  • When: dry, clear months; early morning best. Avoid Jun–Aug & Nov.
  • Bring: binoculars, layers, patience — and ideally a local guide
Tread lightly. Longwood is a fragile, protected Reserve Forest and Ramsar site. Keep to the trail, keep quiet, take nothing, and go with a registered local guide where possible.
