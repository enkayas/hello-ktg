## SECTION: Sholas & Nature (explainer block + Longwood feature)

**Heading:** What's a shola? Kotagiri's living water-towers
**Body:** Sholas are pockets of ancient montane rainforest tucked into the valleys of the high Western Ghats, ringed by natural grassland. Together this "shola–grassland mosaic" works like a giant sponge — soaking up rain and releasing it through perennial streams that feed the villages below.

**Feature — Longwood Shola** · `RAMSAR SITE · 116 HA`
> The last urban shola in the Nilgiris, right in Kotagiri town — a Reserve Forest and Ramsar wetland of international importance. Home to ~40 endemic trees and rare birds like the Nilgiri laughingthrush, Nilgiri blue robin and the vulnerable Nilgiri wood-pigeon. A beginner-friendly guided trail; please tread lightly.
